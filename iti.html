ITI

ITI, which stands for Industrial Training Institute, is a vocational training institution that offers specialized technical education and skill development programs to individuals aspiring to pursue a career in various trades and crafts. Operating under the supervision of the Directorate General of Training (DGT) in India, ITIs provide practical training and theoretical knowledge to equip students with the necessary skills for specific occupations.

These vocational programs typically span from 6 months to 2 years and encompass a wide range of trades, including electrician, plumber, welder, carpenter, computer operator, and more. Students undergo a combination of theoretical instruction and hands-on practical training in workshops or labs, culminating in assessments and examinations. Upon completion, students receive trade-specific certificates recognized by industries and employers, facilitating employment opportunities in both public and private sectors.

ITI graduates can also pursue further education or opt for advanced training programs to expand their career prospects. By bridging the gap between education and industry, ITIs play a crucial role in providing practical skills and enhancing employability for individuals seeking vocational training.
