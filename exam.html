1.UPSC - Union Public Service Commission

The UPSC exam, also known as the Civil Services Examination, is a competitive exam conducted by the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) in India. The exam is conducted annually and is used to select candidates for various positions in the civil services of the Indian government, including the Indian Administrative Service (IAS), Indian Police Service (IPS), and Indian Forest Service (IFS), among others.

The UPSC exam consists of three stages - the Preliminary Exam, the Main Exam, and the Personality Test (Interview). The Preliminary Exam consists of two objective-type papers, while the Main Exam consists of nine papers, eight of which are essay-based and one is language-based. The Personality Test is the final stage of the exam, and candidates who clear this stage are eligible for selection to the civil services of the Indian government.

The UPSC exam is considered to be one of the toughest and most prestigious exams in India, with a very low selection rate. As a result, candidates spend months, and sometimes years, preparing for the exam in order to increase their chances of success.

Here are some key points about the UPSC exam:

  • Eligibility: Candidates must be Indian citizens and have a Bachelor's degree from a recognized university to be eligible to apply for the UPSC exam. The age limit for different categories varies.
  • Exam Pattern: The UPSC exam is conducted in three stages - Preliminary, Main, and Interview. The Preliminary exam consists of two objective-type papers (General Studies and Civil Services Aptitude Test). The Main exam is a written exam consisting of nine papers. The Interview stage is a personality test conducted by the UPSC Board.
  • Recruitment Process: The UPSC exam is conducted for the recruitment of candidates to the Indian Administrative Service (IAS), Indian Police Service (IPS), Indian Foreign Service (IFS), and other Group A and B services.
  • Application Process: The UPSC exam application process is done online on the official website of UPSC.
  • Difficulty Level: The UPSC exam is considered one of the toughest exams in India, with a high level of competition. Candidates have to be well-prepared in general knowledge, current affairs, and aptitude to clear the exam.
  • Preparation: Preparation for the UPSC exam requires long-term planning and consistent effort. Candidates need to be well-versed in the syllabus and current events and should also focus on improving their writing and communication skills.

2.IIT - Indian Institutes of Technology

IIT exam refers to the Joint Entrance Examination (JEE), which is a competitive entrance exam conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) in India. The exam is used to select candidates for admission to undergraduate engineering and architecture programs at the Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs) and other top engineering colleges in India.

The JEE exam is conducted in two stages - JEE Main and JEE Advanced. JEE Main is the first stage of the exam, and it is conducted in online mode. It consists of two papers - Paper 1 for engineering aspirants and Paper 2 for architecture aspirants. The exam tests the candidates' knowledge in subjects like Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics.

Candidates who qualify in JEE Main are eligible to appear for JEE Advanced, which is the second stage of the exam. JEE Advanced is also conducted in online mode and tests the candidates' knowledge in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics, with a greater emphasis on analytical skills and problem-solving abilities.

Here are some key points about the JEE exam:

  • Eligibility: Candidates who have completed their 10+2 (or equivalent) exam from a recognized board with Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics as mandatory subjects are eligible to apply for JEE Main. Only the top 2.5 lakh candidates in JEE Main are eligible to appear for JEE Advanced.
  • Exam Pattern: The JEE Main exam consists of two papers - Paper 1 (for B.E./B.Tech.) and Paper 2 (for B.Arch./B.Planning). JEE Advanced, on the other hand, has two compulsory papers (Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics) and is conducted in online mode.
  • Recruitment Process: JEE Advanced is conducted for admissions to the Bachelor's, Integrated Master's, and Dual Degree programs (entry at the 10+2 level) offered at the Indian Institutes of Technology (IITs). Admissions are also available to other top engineering colleges in India based on JEE Main scores.
  • Application Process: The JEE exam registration and application process is done online on the official website of the National Testing Agency (NTA) for JEE Main and on the official website of IIT for JEE Advanced.
  • Difficulty Level: JEE is one of the toughest entrance exams in India, with a very high level of competition. Candidates have to be well-prepared in Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics to clear the exam.

3.KAS - Karnataka Administrative Service

KAS exam refers to the Karnataka Administrative Service (KAS) exam, which is a competitive exam conducted by the Karnataka Public Service Commission (KPSC) in the Indian state of Karnataka. The exam is used to select candidates for various administrative positions in the state government of Karnataka, including Deputy Superintendent of Police, Assistant Commissioner of Commercial Taxes, and Tahsildar, among others.

The KAS exam consists of three stages - the Preliminary Exam, the Main Exam, and the Personality Test (Interview). The Preliminary Exam consists of two objective-type papers, while the Main Exam consists of eight essay-based papers, which are designed to test the candidates' knowledge in subjects such as Kannada language, English language, General Studies, and their chosen optional subjects.

Candidates who clear the Main Exam are eligible to appear for the Personality Test, which is the final stage of the exam. The Personality Test is conducted to evaluate the candidates' overall personality, communication skills, and suitability for the administrative positions they have applied for.

Here are some key points about the KAS exam:

  • Eligibility: Candidates who have completed a bachelor's degree from a recognized university in India are eligible to apply for the KAS exam. The minimum age limit for the exam is 21 years, and the maximum age limit is 35 years.
  • Exam Pattern: The KAS exam consists of three stages - Preliminary Examination, Main Examination, and Personality Test (Interview). The Preliminary Examination consists of two papers - General Studies and Civil Services Aptitude Test (CSAT). The Main Examination consists of eight papers, including one Kannada language paper, one English language paper, and six General Studies papers.
  • Recruitment Process: Candidates who clear the KAS exam are appointed as Assistant Commissioners, Tahsildars, or Block Development Officers in various districts of Karnataka. After serving for a few years, they can be promoted to higher positions such as Deputy Commissioners, Secretaries, and Chief Secretaries.
  • Application Process: The KAS exam registration and application process is done online on the official website of KPSC. The application process usually starts in January or February and ends in March or April.
  • Eligibility Criteria: Candidates should fulfill certain physical fitness criteria to be eligible for the KAS exam. There are also certain age limits, relaxation, and attempts limits for the KAS exam.

5.IPS - Indian Police Service

IPS exam refers to the Indian Police Service (IPS) exam, which is a competitive exam conducted by the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) in India. The exam is used to select candidates for various positions in the Indian Police Service, which is one of the three All India Services, along with the Indian Administrative Service (IAS) and the Indian Forest Service (IFS).

The IPS exam is conducted in three stages - the Preliminary Exam, the Main Exam, and the Personality Test (Interview). The Preliminary Exam consists of two objective-type papers, while the Main Exam consists of six essay-based papers. The Personality Test is the final stage of the exam, and candidates who clear this stage are eligible for selection to the Indian Police Service.

The IPS exam tests the candidates' knowledge in subjects such as Indian polity, economics, history, geography, and current affairs, as well as their analytical and communication skills. Candidates who clear the exam are appointed as Deputy Superintendent of Police (DSP) in various states of India, and can rise up the ranks to become Superintendent of Police (SP), Deputy Inspector General of Police (DIG), Inspector General of Police (IGP), and Director General of Police (DGP).

Here are some key points about the IPS exam:

  • Eligibility: Candidates who are Indian citizens and have completed a bachelor's degree from a recognized university are eligible to apply for the IPS exam.
  • Exam Pattern: The IPS exam consists of three stages - Preliminary Examination, Main Examination, and Personality Test (Interview). The Preliminary Examination consists of two papers - General Studies and Civil Services Aptitude Test (CSAT). The Main Examination consists of nine papers, including one Indian language paper, one English language paper, and four General Studies papers.
  • Recruitment Process: Candidates who clear the IPS exam are appointed as Assistant Superintendent of Police (ASP) or Deputy Superintendent of Police (DSP) in various states of India. After serving for a few years, they can be promoted to higher positions such as Superintendent of Police (SP), Deputy Inspector General of Police (DIG), Inspector General of Police (IGP), and Director-General of Police (DGP).
  • Application Process: The IPS exam registration and application process is done online on the official website of UPSC. The application process usually starts in February or March and ends in April or May.
  • Eligibility Criteria: Candidates should fulfill certain physical fitness criteria to be eligible for the IPS exam. For example, the minimum height requirement is 165 cm for male candidates and 150 cm for female candidates. There are also certain age limits, relaxation, and attempts limits for the IPS exam.

6.NEET - National Eligibility cum Entrance Test

NEET exam refers to the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET), which is a competitive exam conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) in India. The exam is used for admission to undergraduate medical and dental programs in government and private colleges across India.

NEET exam is conducted in pen-and-paper mode and consists of a single paper of 180 multiple-choice questions. The questions are based on subjects such as Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (Botany and Zoology). The duration of the exam is three hours, and each question carries four marks. There is negative marking of one mark for each incorrect answer.

NEET exam is considered to be one of the most competitive exams in India, with lakhs of students appearing for the exam every year. The exam is conducted in multiple languages to ensure that students from different parts of India can appear for the exam comfortably. The exam is also used for admission to various medical and dental courses in institutions outside India.

Here are some key points about the NEET exam:

  • Eligibility: Candidates who have completed or are appearing in the 10+2 examination with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology/Biotechnology as mandatory subjects are eligible to apply for the NEET exam.
  • Exam Pattern: The NEET exam consists of multiple-choice questions in subjects such as Physics, Chemistry, and Biology/Biotechnology. The exam duration is 3 hours, and there is a negative marking scheme.
  • Participating Institutes: The NEET exam score is accepted by various medical and dental colleges across India for admission to undergraduate courses in MBBS and BDS.
  • Application Process: The NEET exam registration and application process is done online on the official website of NTA. The application process usually starts in December or January and ends in January or February.
  • Admission Procedure: After the NEET exam results are declared, candidates who meet the eligibility criteria and have scored above the cutoff marks set by the participating institutes can apply for admission. The admission procedure may include counseling, document verification, and seat allocation based on the candidate's NEET exam score and other eligibility criteria set by the participating institutes.

11.CLAT - Common Law Admission Test

CLAT exam refers to the Common Law Admission Test, which is a national-level entrance exam conducted in India for admission to undergraduate and postgraduate law courses offered by various law schools and universities across the country.

The CLAT exam is conducted in online mode, and it usually consists of multiple-choice questions. The exam tests the candidates' knowledge in subjects like English language, general knowledge, legal aptitude, logical reasoning, and mathematics, depending on the course they are applying for.

The CLAT exam is conducted once a year, and the cutoff score for admission to various law schools and universities varies from year to year and from institution to institution.

Here are some key points about the CLAT exam:

  • Eligibility: To appear for the CLAT exam, a candidate must have completed their 10+2 examination or an equivalent examination with a minimum of 45% marks (or 40% for reserved category candidates). There is no age limit for appearing in the CLAT exam.
  • Exam Pattern: The CLAT exam is a computer-based test and consists of 150 multiple-choice questions to be answered in two hours. The exam includes five sections, each with 30 questions: English Language, Current Affairs, Legal Reasoning, Logical Reasoning, and Quantitative Techniques.
  • Participating Universities: The CLAT exam score is accepted by 22 National Law Universities (NLUs) across India, including NLUs in cities like Bangalore, Kolkata, Hyderabad, Bhopal, Lucknow, and others.
  • Application Process: Candidates can apply for the CLAT exam online on the official website of the Consortium of National Law Universities. The application process typically starts in January and ends in March.
  • Admission Procedure: After the CLAT results are declared, candidates who meet the eligibility criteria and have scored above the cutoff marks set by the participating universities can apply for admission. The admission procedure may include counseling, document verification, and seat allocation based on the candidate's CLAT score and other eligibility criteria set by the universities.

AILET is one of the most prestigious law entrance exams in India, and qualifying candidates can secure admission to BA LLB (Hons), LLM and PhD programs offered by NLUD.

The exam tests the candidates' aptitude and knowledge in areas such as English, General Knowledge, Legal Aptitude, Reasoning, and Elementary Mathematics. Here are some important aspects of AILET:

For the postgraduate programmes, candidates should have completed their graduation with a minimum of 50% marks.
  • Exam Pattern: The AILET exam is a two-hour long test, comprising 150 multiple-choice questions. The test consists of questions from five different sections, namely English, General Knowledge and Current Affairs, Legal Aptitude, Reasoning, and Mathematics.
  • Participating University: The AILET exam score is accepted by National Law University (NLU), Delhi for admission to its undergraduate (BA LLB) and postgraduate (LLM, PhD) law programmes.
  • Application Process: Candidates can apply for the AILET exam online on the official website of NLU Delhi. The application process typically starts in the month of January and ends in April.
  • Admission Procedure: After the AILET results are declared, candidates who meet the eligibility criteria and have scored above the cutoff marks set by the university can apply for admission. The admission procedure may include counseling, document verification, and seat allocation based on the candidate's AILET score and other eligibility criteria set by the university.
